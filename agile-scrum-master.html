The Challenge

A Fortune 50 Needs
Agile Talent. Fast.

A Fortune 50 global pharmaceutical company needed Agile practitioners and Scrum Masters who could integrate into enterprise delivery teams people capable of running sprints, managing backlogs, facilitating standups, and communicating with senior stakeholders from day one. Their requirement: non-traditional talent. No college degrees required. Diverse candidates currently outside the IT industry.

E

The Employer Requirement

A Fortune 50 pharmaceutical company with a strong diversity commitment needed Agile talent developed from scratch not poached from competitors. They wanted people trained to their specific enterprise environment and team culture from the start.

R

Non-Traditional Recruiting

No IT degrees. No corporate technology experience required. KTCS and its specialty staffing partner sourced candidates from entirely different industries and backgrounds. Identifying aptitude, communication skills, and learning agility over credentials.

The Solution

Design, Recruit,
Deliver, Repeat.

KTCS designed the curriculum, recruited the cohort, and delivered the entire pre-apprenticeship end-to-end. The program ran with daily feedback loops, weekly instructor reports, 1:1 mentoring, and a cohort learning environment that built professional relationships alongside technical skills.

PSM Certification

Professional Scrum Master Pathway

Graduates completed a Professional Scrum Master (PSM) certification pathway a globally recognized credential from Scrum.org that validates their ability to apply the Scrum framework in enterprise environments.

AWS Cloud Practitioner

Cloud Literacy for Agile Teams

AWS Cloud Practitioner certification was embedded into the program curriculum giving graduates working knowledge of cloud infrastructure concepts essential for Agile practitioners operating in modern enterprise technology environments.

100% Conversion

Every Graduate Hired Full-Time

All 18 apprentices completed the 12-month apprenticeship and converted to full-time employment. The result: the same Fortune 50 employer returned to KTCS for a second, technically deeper cohort in Python, AWS, and IT DevOps.

Curriculum Overview

Built for Enterprise
Agile Environments.

The Agile PM and Scrum Master curriculum was designed to align with the Fortune 50 employer's delivery methodology and enterprise toolchain. Graduates entered the 12-month apprenticeship ready to run sprints, facilitate ceremonies, and work directly with senior product stakeholders.

01
Agile Manifesto & Principles
The 12 Agile principles, the Agile manifesto, values-based decision making, Agile vs. Waterfall tradeoffs, and practical application of Agile thinking in enterprise environments.
Agile Foundation
02
Scrum Framework
Scrum roles (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Development Team), events (Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Retrospective), and artifacts (Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment).
PSM Certification Pathway
03
Sprint Planning & Backlog Management
Writing effective user stories, acceptance criteria, story point estimation, sprint velocity, backlog refinement, prioritization techniques, and managing technical debt within sprint cycles.
Product Ownership
04
Stakeholder Communication
Communicating progress to non-technical stakeholders, sprint review facilitation, impediment escalation, managing stakeholder expectations, and reporting in enterprise product environments.
Enterprise Readiness
05
AWS Cloud Practitioner
AWS core services, cloud computing fundamentals, pricing and billing, shared responsibility model, and cloud concepts essential for Agile practitioners in cloud-native enterprise environments.
AWS Cloud Practitioner
06
Capstone & Enterprise Simulation
Full sprint simulation with assigned roles, live backlog management, stakeholder presentation, retrospective facilitation, and final employer readiness assessment before apprenticeship start.
